# Insurance Premium and Claims Analysis using SQL and Power BI

## Overview

This project analyzes insurance customer data to understand premium collection, claim patterns, customer distribution, and policy performance. SQL was used for data analysis and Power BI was used to build dashboards and visualize the results.

## Tools Used

- SQL
- Power BI  
- Microsoft Excel

## Dataset

The dataset contains information about:

- Customer ID
- Age
- Gender
- Region
- Policy Type
- Premium Amount
- Claim Amount
- Policy Status
- Join Date

## Key Insights

### Regional Analysis

- East recorded the highest claim amount (₹15,000), followed by South (₹13,500).
- North and West recorded claim amounts of ₹10,000 and ₹9,000 respectively.
- East generated the highest premium revenue (₹299,000), followed by South (₹292,500).

### Policy Type Analysis

- Auto insurance generated the highest premium amount (₹292,500).
- Travel insurance generated the lowest premium amount (₹270,000).
- Life insurance recorded the highest claim amount (₹15,000).

### Customer Distribution

- East and South each had 13 customers.
- North and West each had 12 customers.
- The dataset contained 25 male and 25 female customers.

### Claim Analysis by Age

Higher claim amounts were observed around ages 22–37 and 52–57.

- Age 22 – ₹6,000
- Age 27 – ₹3,500
- Age 32 – ₹5,000
- Age 37 – ₹9,000
- Age 42 – ₹4,000
- Age 47 – ₹5,500
- Age 52 – ₹10,000
- Age 57 – ₹4,500

### Monthly Trends

- Premium collection was highest during January, February, and March.
- Premium collection dropped in April to ₹82,000 and increased again during the later months.
- No claims were recorded in January.
- March recorded the highest premium amount (₹115,000) and claims of ₹7,000.
- April recorded the lowest premium amount with claims of ₹3,500.

### Policy Status Analysis

- Most policies were active.
- East and South had more active policies.
- North had 11 active policies and only 1 lapsed policy.

### Gender-Based Claim Analysis

- Female customers recorded total claims of ₹25,000.
- Male customers recorded total claims of ₹22,500.

## SQL Concepts Used

- SELECT
- WHERE
- GROUP BY
- HAVING
- ORDER BY
- LIMIT
- Aggregate Functions
- Window Functions (RANK)

## Conclusion

This project helped me gain practical experience with SQL and Power BI. Through this analysis, I was able to identify trends in premiums and claims, compare policy performance, and derive insights from insurance data.
